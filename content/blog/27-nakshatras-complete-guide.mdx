The 27 nakshatras vedic astrology system is the lunar zodiac that complements the 12-sign solar zodiac. Where the 12 rashis give the broad pattern of personality and general life themes, the 27 nakshatras give the specific texture: the subtle energetic signature, the deity association, the specific karmic theme, and the detailed prediction tools that Vedic astrology uses for precise timing. This guide provides an overview of all 27 nakshatras with their essential characteristics.

## Why 27 Nakshatras?

The Moon completes one cycle through the zodiac in approximately 27 days. Each day the Moon transits through one nakshatra (one 13 degree 20 minute portion). The 27 nakshatras therefore represent the 27 stations of the Moon's monthly journey.

Mythologically, the 27 nakshatras are the wives of Chandra (Moon), daughters of Prajapati Daksha. Each has her own personality, gifts, and themes, which Chandra experiences as he transits through her portion of the sky each month.

Practically, the nakshatras are essential to Vedic astrology because they provide:

- The basis for the Vimshottari Mahadasha system
- Detailed birth-time prediction beyond the rashi
- Compatibility analysis through the 8 kootas
- Daily muhurta (auspicious time) calculation
- Karmic insight into specific life themes

## The 27 Nakshatras: Complete List

### 1. Ashwini (0 to 13.20 Aries)

**Lord:** Ketu
**Deity:** Ashwini Kumars (divine twin horsemen, healers)
**Symbol:** Horse's head
**Key trait:** Pioneering, healing, speed

Ashwini natives are initiators with natural healing capacity. They love to be first and bring fresh energy to any field. (See our complete Ashwini guide.)

### 2. Bharani (13.20 to 26.40 Aries)

**Lord:** Venus
**Deity:** Yama (god of death and transformation)
**Symbol:** Yoni (female reproductive organ)
**Key trait:** Creative transformation, fertility, ethical responsibility

Bharani natives carry the energy of profound transformation. They are often morally serious, fertile in creative and material ways, and capable of significant ethical work.

### 3. Krittika (26.40 Aries to 10 Taurus)

**Lord:** Sun
**Deity:** Agni (god of fire)
**Symbol:** Razor or knife
**Key trait:** Cutting through illusion, leadership, fire

Krittika natives have a quality of sharpness and clarity. They cut through what is false to find what is real. Often leaders, teachers, or those who reform institutions.

### 4. Rohini (10 to 23.20 Taurus)

**Lord:** Moon
**Deity:** Brahma (creator)
**Symbol:** Ox cart
**Key trait:** Beauty, abundance, longing

Rohini is the Moon's most beloved nakshatra. Krishna was born here. Natives carry exceptional beauty, creative gifts, and capacity for love. (See our complete Rohini guide.)

### 5. Mrigashira (23.20 Taurus to 6.40 Gemini)

**Lord:** Mars
**Deity:** Soma (Moon as god)
**Symbol:** Deer's head
**Key trait:** Curious seeking, exploration, gentleness

Mrigashira natives are seekers. They explore, search, and refine. Often gentle in manner but persistent in pursuit.

### 6. Ardra (6.40 to 20 Gemini)

**Lord:** Rahu
**Deity:** Rudra (storm and destruction)
**Symbol:** Diamond or teardrop
**Key trait:** Intense transformation through storm

Ardra natives carry storm energy: intense, transformative, sometimes destructive of the old to make way for the new.

### 7. Punarvasu (20 Gemini to 3.20 Cancer)

**Lord:** Jupiter
**Deity:** Aditi (mother of the gods)
**Symbol:** Bow and quiver
**Key trait:** Renewal, return, mother's nurturing

Punarvasu means "return of light." Natives have a quality of starting again, of returning to light after darkness. Strongly nurturing.

### 8. Pushya (3.20 to 16.40 Cancer)

**Lord:** Saturn
**Deity:** Brihaspati (Jupiter as god)
**Symbol:** Cow's udder
**Key trait:** Nourishment, dharma, stability

Pushya is classically the most auspicious nakshatra. Natives are wise, stable, nurturing, dharmic. (See our complete Pushya guide.)

### 9. Ashlesha (16.40 to 30 Cancer)

**Lord:** Mercury
**Deity:** Nagas (serpent deities)
**Symbol:** Coiled serpent
**Key trait:** Depth, magnetism, hidden power

Ashlesha natives have serpentine power: charismatic, deep, sometimes secretive. Often profound healers or transformative teachers.

### 10. Magha (0 to 13.20 Leo)

**Lord:** Ketu
**Deity:** Pitrs (ancestors)
**Symbol:** Royal throne
**Key trait:** Royal nature, ancestor connection, leadership

Magha natives carry royal energy. They are often connected strongly to ancestors, lineage, and traditional authority.

### 11. Purva Phalguni (13.20 to 26.40 Leo)

**Lord:** Venus
**Deity:** Bhaga (god of fortune)
**Symbol:** Front legs of a bed
**Key trait:** Pleasure, creativity, leisure

Purva Phalguni natives love beauty, comfort, and creative expression. They bring joy and abundance to those around them.

### 12. Uttara Phalguni (26.40 Leo to 10 Virgo)

**Lord:** Sun
**Deity:** Aryaman (god of patrons)
**Symbol:** Back legs of a bed
**Key trait:** Patronage, contracts, marriage

Uttara Phalguni natives are excellent in partnership: marriage, business, social contracts. They support others and are supported.

### 13. Hasta (10 to 23.20 Virgo)

**Lord:** Moon
**Deity:** Savitr (Sun as creator)
**Symbol:** Hand
**Key trait:** Skill, dexterity, healing hands

Hasta natives have skilled hands: crafts, healing, intricate work. Practical and reliable.

### 14. Chitra (23.20 Virgo to 6.40 Libra)

**Lord:** Mars
**Deity:** Vishvakarma (divine architect)
**Symbol:** Bright jewel
**Key trait:** Beautiful design, artistic gifts, structure

Chitra natives are creators of beauty in structured form: architects, designers, artists who work with form.

### 15. Swati (6.40 to 20 Libra)

**Lord:** Rahu
**Deity:** Vayu (wind)
**Symbol:** Coral or shoot blown by wind
**Key trait:** Independence, movement, adaptability

Swati natives are independent, mobile, often traveling. They adapt and flow.

### 16. Vishakha (20 Libra to 3.20 Scorpio)

**Lord:** Jupiter
**Deity:** Indra and Agni (king of gods, fire god)
**Symbol:** Triumphal arch or forked branch
**Key trait:** Achievement, focused goal, intensity

Vishakha natives are determined achievers. They pursue goals with intensity and often reach prominent positions.

### 17. Anuradha (3.20 to 16.40 Scorpio)

**Lord:** Saturn
**Deity:** Mitra (god of friendship)
**Symbol:** Lotus
**Key trait:** Devotion, friendship, organized spirituality

Anuradha natives are devoted: in friendship, spiritual practice, and structured work. Often build institutions or communities.

### 18. Jyeshtha (16.40 to 30 Scorpio)

**Lord:** Mercury
**Deity:** Indra (king of gods)
**Symbol:** Earring or amulet
**Key trait:** Seniority, authority, secret knowledge

Jyeshtha natives carry the energy of the eldest: authority, responsibility, secret knowledge. Often leaders in their families and fields.

### 19. Mula (0 to 13.20 Sagittarius)

**Lord:** Ketu
**Deity:** Nirriti (goddess of dissolution)
**Symbol:** Tied roots
**Key trait:** Going to the root, dissolution, spiritual radical change

Mula natives go to the root of things. They dissolve what is unnecessary and reach the essential. Often spiritual seekers.

### 20. Purva Ashadha (13.20 to 26.40 Sagittarius)

**Lord:** Venus
**Deity:** Apas (waters)
**Symbol:** Fan or winnowing basket
**Key trait:** Early victory, invincibility, water energy

Purva Ashadha natives have a quality of invincibility in early efforts. They flow like water and find their way.

### 21. Uttara Ashadha (26.40 Sagittarius to 10 Capricorn)

**Lord:** Sun
**Deity:** Vishvadevas (universal gods)
**Symbol:** Elephant tusk
**Key trait:** Universal victory, lasting achievement

Uttara Ashadha natives build lasting achievements that benefit many. Universally respected.

### 22. Shravana (10 to 23.20 Capricorn)

**Lord:** Moon
**Deity:** Vishnu
**Symbol:** Ear or three footprints
**Key trait:** Listening, learning, spiritual scholarship

Shravana natives are listeners and learners. Often scholars, teachers, or those who carry traditional knowledge.

### 23. Dhanishta (23.20 Capricorn to 6.40 Aquarius)

**Lord:** Mars
**Deity:** Eight Vasus (gods of light)
**Symbol:** Drum
**Key trait:** Music, rhythm, group leadership

Dhanishta natives are rhythmic: musicians, dancers, group leaders who organize energy through pattern.

### 24. Shatabhisha (6.40 to 20 Aquarius)

**Lord:** Rahu
**Deity:** Varuna (god of cosmic waters)
**Symbol:** Empty circle or 100 healers
**Key trait:** Mystery, healing, isolation

Shatabhisha natives are mysterious healers. Often work with the hidden, isolated, or marginalized.

### 25. Purva Bhadrapada (20 Aquarius to 3.20 Pisces)

**Lord:** Jupiter
**Deity:** Aja Ekapada (one-footed goat)
**Symbol:** Front legs of a funeral bed
**Key trait:** Spiritual intensity, transformation, dualism

Purva Bhadrapada natives carry intense spiritual energy. Often work with transformation at deep levels.

### 26. Uttara Bhadrapada (3.20 to 16.40 Pisces)

**Lord:** Saturn
**Deity:** Ahir Budhnya (serpent of the deep)
**Symbol:** Back legs of a funeral bed
**Key trait:** Deep wisdom, occult knowledge, completion

Uttara Bhadrapada natives carry deep, ancient wisdom. Often work in spiritual or occult fields.

### 27. Revati (16.40 to 30 Pisces)

**Lord:** Mercury
**Deity:** Pushan (nourishing Sun)
**Symbol:** Fish or drum
**Key trait:** Nourishment, journey, completion

Revati natives carry the energy of safe journey and nourishment. Often supportive figures, teachers of children, or those who help others complete their journeys.

## The Vimshottari Dasha and Nakshatras

Your birth nakshatra determines your Vimshottari Mahadasha sequence:

- Ashwini, Magha, Mula nakshatras: Ketu Mahadasha at birth
- Bharani, Purva Phalguni, Purva Ashadha: Venus Mahadasha at birth
- Krittika, Uttara Phalguni, Uttara Ashadha: Sun Mahadasha at birth
- Rohini, Hasta, Shravana: Moon Mahadasha at birth
- Mrigashira, Chitra, Dhanishta: Mars Mahadasha at birth
- Ardra, Swati, Shatabhisha: Rahu Mahadasha at birth
- Punarvasu, Vishakha, Purva Bhadrapada: Jupiter Mahadasha at birth
- Pushya, Anuradha, Uttara Bhadrapada: Saturn Mahadasha at birth
- Ashlesha, Jyeshtha, Revati: Mercury Mahadasha at birth

The exact Mahadasha activation time depends on the precise degree of the Moon within the nakshatra at birth.
