Ashtakavarga in Vedic astrology is one of the most powerful but underutilized predictive tools. It assigns numerical "bindus" (auspicious points) to each sign of the zodiac based on contributions from the seven main planets and the ascendant. The result is a numerical map of strength across the 12 signs, used to predict transit results, refine timing, and assess the chart's overall capacity in different areas.

This guide explains what ashtakavarga is, how the bindus are assigned, how to read the score, and how to use it for practical prediction.

## What is Ashtakavarga?

Ashtakavarga literally means "eight divisions" in Sanskrit. The system divides the chart into eight contributing factors: the seven main planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) and the ascendant. Each contributor assigns auspicious bindus to each of the 12 signs from its natal position, following classical rules.

The result is a matrix of bindus that reveals which signs are strong (high bindus) and which are weak (low bindus) for the native. When planets transit through these signs, the bindu strength of the sign predicts the quality of the transit results.

## How Bindus Are Assigned

Each of the eight contributors assigns bindus to specific houses (signs counted from itself) according to classical rules in Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra. Some examples:

**Sun's contribution:** Sun assigns auspicious bindus when located at certain distances from itself. The 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th, 9th, 10th, and 11th houses from Sun receive Sun's bindu. Other houses do not.

**Moon's contribution:** Moon assigns bindus at the 3rd, 6th, 7th, 8th, 10th, and 11th from itself, among others.

Each planet has its specific table of contributions. The cumulative result for each sign across all eight contributors gives the Sarvashtakavarga score for that sign.

## Reading the Sarvashtakavarga Score

Sarvashtakavarga (SAV) is the total of all eight contributors per sign. The scores range approximately from 25 to 38 across the 12 signs of a typical chart.

**Interpretation framework:**

- **Score above 30:** Sign is energetically strong. Transits through this sign tend to produce favorable results, especially for the houses that fall here.
- **Score 25 to 30:** Average. Mixed results during transits, requiring deeper analysis.
- **Score below 25:** Sign is energetically weak. Transits here can produce difficult results, particularly malefic planet transits.

The highest scoring sign in the chart often corresponds to areas of life where the native experiences ease and natural support. The lowest scoring sign often corresponds to areas of recurring difficulty.

## Individual Bhinnashtakavarga

While Sarvashtakavarga aggregates all contributors, Bhinnashtakavarga (individual planetary ashtakavarga) provides the contribution of each single planet. For specific predictions:

**Saturn's individual ashtakavarga:** Used to assess the strength of Saturn transits, particularly during Sade Sati or Saturn Mahadasha. High bindus from Saturn for a sign mean Saturn's transit there is supported. Low bindus mean caution.

**Jupiter's individual ashtakavarga:** Used to assess Jupiter transits, particularly important for marriage, children, dharma, and learning. High Jupiter bindus on the 7th house support marriage timing during Jupiter transit there.

**Sun's individual ashtakavarga:** Used for career and authority transits.

A complete analysis uses both SAV and the individual planetary ashtakavargas relevant to the question.

## Using Ashtakavarga for Career Predictions

For career timing predictions, the 10th house ashtakavarga score matters significantly:

- A 10th house with high Sarvashtakavarga (above 30) suggests career strength and supportive transits
- Saturn's bindus on the 10th house predict the strength of Saturn's career-related transits
- Jupiter's bindus on the 10th house predict the strength of Jupiter's career-related transits

A consultation might say: "Your 10th house has SAV of 33 with 5 Saturn bindus. Saturn's transit through your 10th in 2027 is well-supported, suggesting a career advancement window."

## Using Ashtakavarga for Marriage Predictions

For marriage timing, the 7th house ashtakavarga matters:

- A 7th house with high SAV supports marriage during favorable transits
- Venus and Jupiter individual bindus on the 7th predict the strength of these planets' transits for marriage
- A 7th house with low SAV (under 25) suggests challenges in marriage timing or quality

## Ashtakavarga and Sade Sati

Sade Sati (Saturn's 7.5 year transit) effects vary significantly depending on Saturn's bindus through the 12th, 1st, and 2nd from the natal Moon:

- High Saturn bindus on these signs: Sade Sati is structurally productive, bringing lasting career or property gains
- Low Saturn bindus on these signs: Sade Sati requires more careful navigation, with stronger remedies recommended

This is one reason that two natives experiencing Sade Sati simultaneously can have very different experiences: the bindu pattern is different.

## The Trikona Reduction Method

A specialized ashtakavarga technique called Trikona Shodhana reduces the bindus by examining the trine (5th and 9th) relationships from each sign. This refinement can reveal hidden strengths or weaknesses not visible in raw SAV.

A consultation might apply Trikona Shodhana to refine the predictions further. This is the kind of detailed analysis that distinguishes serious Vedic practice from generic interpretation.

## Ashtakavarga in Software

All modern Vedic astrology software calculates ashtakavarga instantly. The output shows the SAV matrix, individual planetary ashtakavargas, and sometimes the reduction techniques.

What software does not do is interpret the results in context: which houses matter for which questions, how the bindu pattern integrates with dasha, what the practical implications are for life decisions. This requires the trained Jyotishi.

## Common Misuses of Ashtakavarga

Some misuses to avoid:

**Using only SAV without checking individual planetary contributions:** SAV gives the aggregate, but the relevant planet's individual bindus often matter more.

**Ignoring sign strength of the planet:** A planet with high bindus in a sign where it is debilitated still struggles.

**Using ashtakavarga without dasha:** Bindus tell you the strength of transits, but the active dasha tells you which themes are activated.

**Applying it generically:** A 7th house SAV of 30 means one thing for a single native exploring marriage; it means something different for a married native exploring relationship dynamics.
