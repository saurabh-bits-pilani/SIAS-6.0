Rohini nakshatra compatibility is one of the most common compatibility queries because Rohini is the Moon's most beloved nakshatra and is considered exceptionally important for marriage matching in Vedic tradition. This guide covers how compatibility works for Rohini natives, the best and worst matches according to classical analysis, how the 8 kootas of kundli matching apply specifically to Rohini, and what to do when the analysis shows incompatibility.

## How Nakshatra Compatibility Works in Vedic Astrology

Vedic compatibility analysis (called Ashtakuta Milan or 8-fold matching) compares the nakshatras of the prospective bride and groom across eight criteria, each scored numerically. The total of all eight scores out of 36 indicates compatibility:

- 18 to 24: Compatible
- 24 to 32: Very compatible
- 32 to 36: Highly compatible
- Below 18: Generally not recommended without strong supporting factors elsewhere in the charts

The eight kootas are:

1. **Varna (1 point):** Spiritual compatibility
2. **Vashya (2 points):** Mutual influence
3. **Tara (3 points):** Health and well-being
4. **Yoni (4 points):** Sexual and instinctual compatibility
5. **Graha Maitri (5 points):** Mental compatibility
6. **Gana (6 points):** Temperamental compatibility
7. **Bhakoot (7 points):** Family welfare
8. **Nadi (8 points):** Physical compatibility and progeny

For Rohini nakshatra, the koota assignments are: Varna = Vaishya, Vashya = Chatushpada (four-footed), Yoni = Snake, Gana = Manushya (human), Nadi = Antya (last).

These assignments determine how Rohini scores against each other nakshatra in compatibility analysis.

## Rohini Nakshatra Best Matches

### Mrigashira

Mrigashira and Rohini share both the Taurus rashi (Mrigashira begins in Taurus and ends in Gemini). The two nakshatras are adjacent and the energetic transition is smooth. Mrigashira natives bring a curiosity and gentleness that complements Rohini's depth without overwhelming it.

Compatibility score with Rohini: typically high (24-30+ out of 36).

### Hasta

Hasta nakshatra falls in Virgo and shares an earthy practicality with Rohini's Taurus. The combination produces grounded, steady relationships with strong domestic foundations. Hasta natives are skilled and reliable, matching Rohini's appreciation for craft and beauty in daily life.

Compatibility score with Rohini: high (22-28).

### Uttara Phalguni

Uttara Phalguni falls in Leo-Virgo and is ruled by the Sun. The Sun's warmth and structure pair well with Rohini's Moon emotional intelligence. The combination tends to be stable, family-oriented, and supportive.

Compatibility score with Rohini: high (24-30).

### Anuradha

Anuradha is ruled by Saturn and falls in Scorpio. Despite the very different lord (Saturn vs Moon), the matches often work because Anuradha brings discipline and emotional depth that supports Rohini's nurturing nature without conflict.

Compatibility score: moderate to high (20-26).

### Another Rohini (with conditions)

Two Rohini natives can produce exceptional matches when the rest of the chart supports. Both share the same depth, the same aesthetic sensibility, and the same capacity for devotion. The risk is that both share the same shadow: possessiveness. Conscious work on spaciousness is essential.

Compatibility score: variable, depending on chart specifics (typically 22-30).

## Rohini Nakshatra Worst Matches

### Jyestha

Jyestha is ruled by Mercury and falls in Scorpio. The Bhakoot (family welfare) score between Rohini and Jyestha is often low because of the 2-12 axis between Taurus and Scorpio rashi positions. The match can produce tension around family matters, finances, and emotional pace differences.

### Mula

Mula is ruled by Ketu and falls in Sagittarius. The energy of Mula is detachment and spiritual seeking; Rohini's energy is attachment and material abundance. The combination requires significant effort to harmonize. Gana koota also tends to be poor since Mula falls in Rakshasa gana while Rohini is Manushya.

### Ashlesha

Ashlesha is ruled by Mercury and falls in Cancer. While Cancer and Taurus are usually compatible (5-9 or 9-5 axis), Ashlesha brings a more serpentine, secretive quality that can create distance with Rohini's openness. Nadi koota issues sometimes apply.

These nakshatras are not absolute exclusions. A complete chart analysis including the 7th house condition, dasha sequence, and navamsha can show whether the match is workable despite low nakshatra koota score.

## The 8 Kootas Applied to Rohini

When matching Rohini with any other nakshatra, the Jyotishi assesses each koota:

**Varna:** Rohini is Vaishya (merchant/cultivator class). Best matches are with Vaishya or higher (Brahmin, Kshatriya).

**Vashya:** Rohini is Chatushpada (four-footed nature, associated with Taurus). Matches with other Chatushpada nakshatras score well.

**Tara:** Tara is calculated by counting nakshatras from the groom's nakshatra to the bride's (and vice versa). Auspicious Taras are 3, 5, 7 (counted differently). The mathematical calculation gives a binary result (compatible or not).

**Yoni:** Rohini's yoni is Snake. Snake yoni pairs best with Mongoose (its complement) and avoids natural predator-prey conflicts.

**Graha Maitri:** Compatibility of the rashi lords. Rohini's rashi is Taurus, ruled by Venus. Venus's friendly planets (Mercury, Saturn) produce favorable Graha Maitri.

**Gana:** Rohini is Manushya gana. Best matches are with other Manushya gana nakshatras.

**Bhakoot:** The relative positions of the two Moons. 6-8 axis and 2-12 axis are unfavorable; 1-7 and 5-9 are favorable.

**Nadi:** Rohini is Antya nadi. Same nadi matches are traditionally avoided (Nadi dosha), though this rule is sometimes relaxed for specific exceptions.

## Can Incompatible Nakshatras Marry?

The classical answer is qualified yes. A low koota score is a warning to examine other factors carefully, not an absolute prohibition. Marriages with low koota scores have succeeded when:

- The 7th house and its lord are strong in both charts
- The navamsha (D9) chart shows compatibility even when the rashi chart does not
- The dasha sequence of both partners aligns to support marriage during the same time period
- Major dosha cancellations apply
- Both partners have strong Venus and Jupiter

Conversely, marriages with high koota scores have struggled when:

- One partner has severe Mangal Dosha or Kaal Sarp Dosha that is not cancelled
- The 7th house lord is debilitated in one or both charts
- Dasha sequences place one partner in a difficult period during the early marriage years
- The navamsha chart shows tension that the rashi chart hides

The conclusion is that nakshatra compatibility is one important factor but never the only factor.
